Trees Glorious Trees!

Did you know that trees are the largest and longest living plants on Earth?

They reproduce by seeds and have flowers. They absorb carbon dioxide and produce oxygen, keeping our air fresh.

Vancouver has at least 30 different kinds of street trees!

Ranging from giant sequoiadendron, elm, maple, chestnut, oak and liriodendron to robinia, ash, hawthorn, birch, linden, beech and many more1.
The Kwanzan Flowering Cherry tree is the most common tree in Vancouver with 10,388 planted across the city2. Try to check out the cherry trees blossoming in late March - early April.
